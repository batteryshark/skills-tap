# Interview and reconciliation

## Recovering rationale

Inspect available evidence before interviewing. Ask one narrow question at a time when its answer can change the record. Useful prompts include:

- Which constraint eliminated otherwise attractive alternatives?
- What behavior or compatibility requirement must remain intact?
- Which option was tried, rejected, or deferred, and what evidence drove that result?
- What surprising part is intentional or load-bearing?
- What future condition would make a different choice preferable?

Capture an answer close to the speaker's wording, then record interpretation separately. Do not turn silence, memory gaps, or a plausible implementation pattern into attributed intent.

## Reconciling a record with reality

Compare the record with current requirements, implementation, tests, operations, and later decisions.

- **Keep:** the decision and its constraints still describe reality.
- **Amend:** the decision is intact, but evidence, consequences, or clarifications changed.
- **Supersede:** a later decision replaced the direction. Preserve the original and link both ways.
- **Deprecate:** the decision no longer governs because the affected capability was removed.
- **Investigate:** important drift exists, but current intent or behavior cannot be established safely.

Do not rewrite an old record to make it appear prescient. Add a dated note or successor that distinguishes original context from later knowledge.
