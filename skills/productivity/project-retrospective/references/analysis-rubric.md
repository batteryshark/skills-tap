# Analysis rubric

## Goal and scope

- What was the original objective?
- What constraints and success conditions were known?
- When did the goal, scope, or priority change?

## Outcomes

- What concrete artifacts, decisions, or verified results were produced?
- What remains incomplete, unverified, or intentionally deferred?
- Did the final result satisfy the latest agreed goal?

## Effective practices

Look for actions worth repeating: early inspection, narrow experiments, good verification, clear decisions, reversible changes, useful collaboration, and fast correction after new evidence.

## Friction and rework

Identify wrong assumptions, missing context, tool or environment mistakes, premature implementation, unclear scope, weak validation, and repeated failures. Explain how the issue was detected and its cost.

## Decisions and pivots

Record the evidence available, the choice made, alternatives rejected, and whether the decision still looks sound. A pivot can be good process when new evidence justified it.

## Communication

When phrasing materially affected the outcome, state:

1. the instruction or missing context
2. the reasonable interpretation at the time
3. a more precise reusable formulation

Use this as coordination improvement, not blame.

## Improvements

Prefer actions that change future behavior:

- a verification step
- a clearer default or boundary
- a reusable skill or script
- earlier evidence collection
- a smaller commit or experiment
- a decision that should be documented once

Each improvement needs an owner or trigger and a condition for knowing it worked.
